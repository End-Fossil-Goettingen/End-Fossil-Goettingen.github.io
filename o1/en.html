In short: What we take away

- ! Plan everything as well as possible before the occupation, because the occupation is more emotionally and physically exhausting than expected: little light, many people in one room, few possibilities to retreat, overtiredness-> for this you need breaks, possibilities to relax, good planning of the time in the occupation and a good atmosphere
- Good structures need to be organized before the occupation and regular onboardings for people who want to help organize are also needed during the occupation to involve as many people as possible and to relieve people who do a lot of work

Occupation and Talk

What did we do?

- Planned a lecture in the biggest university lecture hall and then occupied a lecture hall from Monday to Friday and ended with a rally.

It worked well

- The lecture was attended by many people
- Many people spontaneously stayed after the lecture
- Remained in discussion with the university, so that occupation was not directly dissolved
- Setting up in the lecture hall, bringing sleeping bags, food, other material directly to the lecture hall after the lecture
- There were always people in the lecture hall and they also slept there.- Possibility to leave the lecture hall, to use the toilets in the university building

Did not work out so well

- After the lecture on Monday, involve the people who stayed there.
- Forming reference groups directly after the lecture

We will take this with us

- more detailed plan and program after the lecture, to be able to involve people spontaneously

Mobilization

What have we done?

- Started in August with a few people and mobilized in leftist groups in Göttingen-> but much of the orga happened in the weeks before the occupation
- printed flyers and distributed them at the Global Strike
- several open meetings before the occupation
- an action plenum shortly before the occupation with a detailed description of what the plan is

Worked well:

- Mobilization for lecture through posters, flyers, social media- Partly cooperation with professors and lectures, which should have taken place in the lecture hall, brings new people to the event.

Did not work so well

- Contact to other groups from Göttingen
- More orderly communication between infochannel and staffing
- Too few people for mobi on campus during occupation
- People who came to the lecture hall out of curiosity were not welcomed enough: Orga workshop at the beginning

What we take away:

- It is important to have an info booth and if there are always people to talk the kapas with people who are curious and spontaneously drop by
- The auditorium needs to look like an open space to the outside world, because many people imagine a cast as something different than an open space with a program
- Regular onboardings are needed to get more people involved in the orga- People should start flyers or other mobi actions on campus so that more people get to know about the occupation

Program

What did we do?

- There was a program with lectures and workshops in the occupied lecture hall

Worked well

- Action plenum Saturday gave people more security about what is planned
- Common "early morning sport" at the start of the morning plenum made people awake
- Panel discussion with university president Tolan
- Fishbowl discussion on socialization of energy companies
- Student plenary meeting as participatory event
- Organizing workshop
- Depth for Climate lecture
- Lützi lecture

Didn't work out so well:

- better, firmer daily planning
- clear rest times
- Rave would have been cool, but unfortunately did not work out 
- Program early in the morning difficult
- program points too serious: also fun/creative ones
- program changed too often, too spontaneous

We will take this with us

- Set the program the night before and make it public- plan breaks
- less program points, but include possible time slots for openspaces

Demands and goals

What did we do?

- supported international demands and worked out our own local demands during the occupation- understood the lecture hall as an open space where other perspectives should find room and where all people can get involved and organize themselves
- understood the action as a repoliticization of universities

Worked well

- Talks with the university, university has accepted almost all local demands

Did not work so well

- Communication about occupation as an open space and as more than just demands to the university- Joint elaboration of local demands
- Communication of international demands

We will take with us

- Put international demands more in the foreground, for example via flyers, more posters, more events about it (for example: Why do we occupy the lecture hall?)
- Event for the elaboration of local demands

Press and social media

What did we do?

An infochannel on Telegram and an Insta profile, gave press interviews and wrote press releases.

Worked well

- Press has reported a lot

Didn't work out so well

- Clear narrative- Why is the auditorium being occupied? Question needs to be answered more publicly/better, perhaps with introduction- Prepare social media better beforehand
- Few people wanted to give press interviews

We will take this with us

- Press training before and during occupation
- Clear narrative describing why we are occupying a lecture hall against occupation clichés

Structure and Organization

What did we do?

- Beforehand we founded groups and AKs for logistics, program, mobi, social media, awareness.

Worked well

- Spontaneous things still worked well

Did not work so well

- Quick decision making in plenums- clear structure
- more transparent decision making process
- clearer responsibilities
- Longer plenary sessions problematic
- Meetings of the AKs
- Much burden on few people-knowledge transfer (e.g. what the previous plenum decided)

What we take with us

- Need better defined tasks so more people know what who can take onSustainable activism, awareness and de-escalation-----------------------------------

What did we do.

An awareness and de-escalation concept and shift plan, a BIPoC and Flinta safer space. Our awareness concept in english: https://pad.riseup.net/p/awareness_goettingen-keep

Worked well

- Good atmosphere, people felt comfortable most of the time

Did not work so well

- Clear rest periods- Shifts partly not occupied
- Late planning of the awareness concept and de-escalation, some questions about this not clarified in advance
- Safer spaces not properly demarcated and used
- a place of retreat is missing

From this we take with us

An occupation costs a lot of energy, physically and emotionally, so it is important that there is space for activists to rest.De-escalation training and awareness training in advance and during the occupation to involve and empower more people.- Awareness    - Plan early, occupy shift plan in advance   
- Communicate the concept well during the occupation   
- Step-by-step plan Awareness    - Low-threshold contact possibilities Awareness team
- sustainable activism    - little sleep must not be heroicized   
- Recreation opportunities: Place for rest and firmly scheduled breaks, also ideas for activities in the occupied place and outside of it- De-escalation   
- Clarify before occupation: Ethical understanding: how do we deal with people who are disruptive? Which people do we not want to talk to? What is threatening and how?

Open space for all

What did we do?

Intended the occupation to be a place where everyone could help create and participate

Worked well

- Have information material ready- No known corona cases
- Respectful interaction with each other
- loving community
- feeling of welcome
- no Corona
- nothing lost
- no encroaching situations
- good that we could go in and out and also retreat

did not work out so well

- info table and material from the beginning
- offers in english
- Onboarding, how to activism
- Involve people from outside more

What we take with us

- Make occupation more inviting and approach people more
